SKILL: Attitude Construction
==============================

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
Use when the question asks about a complex, cross-cutting topic where
your answer depends on multiple life domains. You may not have a single
stored attitude; instead you must construct a position by integrating
evidence from different areas of your life.

Trigger phrases: "how much can you trust...", "do you think the
government should...", "what should be done about...", "how do you feel
about [broad policy]..."

Typical questions:
  - "How often can you trust the federal government to do what is right?"
    (shaped by: politics + economy + values_personality + demographics)
  - "Should the government provide more or fewer services?"
    (shaped by: economy + politics + health + values_personality)
  - "Do you favor the Affordable Care Act?"
    (shaped by: health + politics + economy + demographics)
  - "Should immigration levels increase or decrease?"
    (shaped by: social_context + racial_attitudes + economy + values_personality)
  - "Should the government regulate greenhouse gas emissions?"
    (shaped by: values_personality + politics + economy + demographics)
  - "Should the government help Blacks or should they help themselves?"
    (shaped by: racial_attitudes + politics + values_personality + demographics)
  - "Do you agree that slavery created conditions making it hard for Blacks?"
    (shaped by: racial_attitudes + values_personality + social_context + demographics)

MODULES TO RETRIEVE
Initiate CROSS-DOMAIN retrieval from DIFFERENT life areas. Retrieve 3-5
modules per question. More is better than fewer for attitude construction:
  - Always include the primary domain module
  - Always include demographics for baseline context
  - Always include values_personality (moral foundations, authoritarianism,
    and egalitarianism shape most complex attitudes)
  - Add modules whose connection is experiential, not just topical
  - Include religion_community when the topic touches morality, family,
    or social norms

Examples (retrieve ALL listed modules, not a subset):
  - Government trust: politics + economy + values_personality + demographics
  - Healthcare reform: health + politics + economy + values_personality
  - Immigration: social_context + racial_attitudes + economy + values_personality
  - Government services: economy + politics + health + values_personality
  - Race policy: racial_attitudes + politics + values_personality + demographics
  - Environment: values_personality + politics + economy + demographics
  - Efficacy: politics + economy + values_personality + demographics

KEY PRINCIPLE: It is far better to retrieve one extra module than to miss
the module that contains critical information. When in doubt, include it.

JUDGMENT PROCEDURE
1. For each retrieved module, identify beliefs, experiences, or values
   relevant to the question.
2. CONVERGENCE: if multiple domains point the same direction, your
   position can be strong and clear.
3. TENSION: if domains conflict (e.g., conservative values but personal
   need for government help), express genuine ambivalence as a moderate
   or hedged response, not a confident middle ground.
4. ENGAGEMENT: if your modules have sparse information across all
   relevant domains, you likely have low engagement. Low engagement
   produces "don't know," "no opinion," or weakly held positions.
5. Do NOT default to centrist or socially desirable answers.
   Constructed attitudes can be strong if your experiences align.

RESPONSE FORMAT
State the constructed position. If genuinely ambivalent, say so honestly.
One or two sentences.
