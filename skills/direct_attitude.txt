SKILL: Direct Attitude Report
===============================

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
Use when the question asks about a specific social or policy attitude
where you likely already hold a clear position. These are "what do you
think about X" questions about a single topic.

Trigger phrases: "do you favor or oppose...", "do you agree or disagree
that...", "should [policy] be...", "how much do you support..."

Typical questions:
  - "Do you favor or oppose the death penalty?"
  - "Should it be easier or harder to buy a gun?"
  - "Do you agree: 'If Blacks would only try harder...'"
  - "Should same-sex couples be allowed to marry?"
  - "Do you agree: 'Newer lifestyles are contributing to breakdown...'"
  - "How much discrimination is there against Blacks?"

MODULES TO RETRIEVE
Retrieve 2-3 topic-relevant modules PLUS demographics (so 3-4 total).
Attitudes are rarely shaped by a single domain. Always include the
primary domain AND at least one supporting domain:

  - Political/government attitudes:
      politics + values_personality + demographics
  - Social issue attitudes (guns, death penalty):
      social_context + values_personality + demographics
  - Marriage/family/lifestyle questions:
      social_context + religion_community + values_personality + demographics
  - Race-related attitudes (discrimination, racial resentment):
      racial_attitudes + values_personality + demographics
  - Race-related policy (government aid to Blacks):
      racial_attitudes + politics + values_personality + demographics
  - Abortion:
      social_context + religion_community + values_personality + demographics
  - Values/moral questions (lifestyles, tradition):
      values_personality + religion_community + demographics

KEY PRINCIPLE: values_personality and religion_community are cross-cutting.
They shape positions on nearly every social and moral issue. When in doubt,
include them. It is far better to retrieve one extra module than to miss
the module that contains the answer.

JUDGMENT PROCEDURE
1. Find the stored attitude or value closest to the question topic.
2. If a recorded position maps directly to one response option, select it.
3. If the question is narrower than your stored position, use the
   stored attitude as an anchor and adjust minimally.
4. If multiple modules contain relevant information, look for
   CONVERGENCE across them to determine the strength of the position.
5. Report at the strength your profile indicates. Do NOT moderate
   strong or unpopular views. A conservative persona opposes; a
   liberal persona supports. Do not flatten.
6. CRITICAL: Do not assume attitudes from demographics alone.
   A Black person can favor the death penalty. A Democrat can oppose
   immigration. Read the actual module data, not stereotypes.

RESPONSE FORMAT
State the answer that matches your stored attitude. One or two sentences.
