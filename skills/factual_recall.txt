SKILL: Factual Recall & Estimation
====================================

WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
Use when the question asks about your concrete personal circumstances,
lived experiences, or requires estimation from your actual situation.

Trigger phrases: "are you...", "have you...", "would you say you...",
"has [something] gotten better or worse for you", "how worried are you",
"do you have [insurance/job/etc.]"

Typical questions:
  - "Are you better off or worse off financially than a year ago?"
  - "Would you say the nation's economy has gotten better or worse?"
  - "How worried are you about your financial situation?"
  - "Do you have health insurance?"

MODULES TO RETRIEVE
Retrieve from concrete-experience modules. Include 2-3 modules + demographics:
  - Financial/economic situation: economy + life_narrative + demographics
  - Health/body/insurance: health + economy + demographics
  - Personal circumstances: life_narrative + economy + demographics
  - National economy assessment: economy + politics + demographics
  - Local conditions: local_context + economy + demographics
  - Media/information habits: media_consumption + demographics
  - Religious practice: religion_community + demographics
Include life_narrative when the question asks about personal changes over
time, as it provides context for interpreting economic or health data.

JUDGMENT PROCEDURE
1. Locate specific facts in the retrieved modules (e.g., income level,
   insurance status, financial worry level, employment status, health
   conditions, food security, housing status).
2. If a direct fact answers the question, report it straightforwardly.
3. If only indirect evidence exists, estimate conservatively from what
   you have. Sparse information signals low salience: respond with
   middle-of-scale or "about the same" rather than strong positions.
4. Ground your answer in your actual circumstances. If you are
   struggling financially, say so. If your health is poor, report
   that honestly. Do not sanitize your reality.

RESPONSE FORMAT
State your answer using the offered scale/options. One or two sentences.
